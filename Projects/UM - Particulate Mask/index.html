Awarded "Best Senior Design Concept."
Invented reusable, durable filtration mask to prevent chronic lung disease in military deployed in desert environments.

University of Massachusetts Amherst
B.S. Mechanical Engineering
2014 - 2018

Overview

Development of an advanced particulate filtration mask balancing filtration efficiency, breathability, and user comfort.

Problem Statement

Traditional masks provide poor filtration or are uncomfortable for extended wear. Challenge: design a mask offering superior protection without sacrificing user comfort and breathability.

Solution & Approach

Performed material selection studies on filter media, designed ergonomic fit validation, and tested filtration performance using standard testing protocols.

Results & Outcomes

Successfully developed a mask design offering 95%+ filtration efficiency while maintaining comfortable wear for extended periods.

Key Learnings

Personal protective equipment design requires balancing competing requirements. User testing revealed that comfort is as important as protection for adoption.
