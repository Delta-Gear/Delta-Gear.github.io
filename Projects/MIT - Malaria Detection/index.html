Overview

Development of AI models for automated malaria parasite detection in blood smear microscopy images for faster diagnosis in developing regions.

Problem Statement

Manual diagnosis is time-consuming and depends on expert availability. Challenge: develop AI models that reliably detect parasites with limited training data.

Solution & Approach

Implemented CNNs with transfer learning and data augmentation to overcome limited labeled data. Models optimized for deployment on mobile devices.

Results & Outcomes

Achieved detection accuracy comparable to expert microscopists while maintaining computational efficiency for mobile deployment.

Key Learnings

Medical AI requires careful attention to accuracy, reliability, deployment constraints, and ethical considerations with domain expert validation.
