Overview

Development of computer vision and deep learning capabilities for accurate 6D pose estimation of objects in robotic manipulation tasks.

Problem Statement

Precise object pose estimation is critical for robotic grasping. Traditional methods fail with lighting and occlusion. Challenge: develop robust deep learning models for real robot environments.

Solution & Approach

Implemented CNNs trained on synthetic and real data for 6D pose estimation. System integrates RGB-D camera input with real-time inference for robotic control.

Results & Outcomes

Achieved real-time pose estimation with high accuracy, enabling successful robotic grasping across diverse objects and scenarios.

Key Learnings

Domain adaptation and synthetic-to-real transfer remain critical. Continuous refinement and real-world testing were essential for practical deployment.
