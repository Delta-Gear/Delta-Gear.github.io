My Journey

I started my career with a degree in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Massachusetts Amherst, where I developed a strong foundation in design, analysis, and prototyping. This education instilled in me a problem-solving mindset that drives everything I do.

After graduating in 2018, I joined Lenze Americas as a Technical Solutions Engineer, where I gained invaluable experience in industrial automation and troubleshooting complex systems. This role taught me the importance of understanding customer needs and delivering reliable solutions.

Product Engineering at Boston Micromachines

In 2020, I transitioned to Boston Micromachines as a Senior Product Engineer, where I've led significant portions of the product development cycle. My responsibilities have included:

  • Hardware Design: Designed and optimized optical and mechanical systems for precision micro-devices
  • Manufacturing: Developed manufacturing processes and automation solutions, reducing cycle times and improving quality
  • Cost Reduction: Identified opportunities for design optimization and supplier partnering, achieving significant cost reductions
  • Leadership: Mentored junior engineers and led cross-functional teams to deliver complex projects

Continuous Learning

In 2023, I completed a Certificate in Applied Data Science from MIT, furthering my technical toolkit to include machine learning and data analysis. This opened new possibilities for applying computational techniques to engineering problems.

Currently, I'm pursuing a Master's degree in Robotics & Autonomous Systems at Boston University, diving deeper into robotics perception, control systems, and autonomous systems design. This combination of practical industry experience and formal education positions me uniquely to tackle complex engineering challenges.
